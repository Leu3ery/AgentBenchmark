Summary (concise)

1) Most recent enforcement milestone
- The most recent major milestone that came into force was the start of the rules for General‑Purpose AI (GPAI) and related governance duties on 2 August 2025; the Commission published a voluntary General‑Purpose AI Code of Practice on 10 July 2025 to support that wave. ([eur-lex.europa.eu](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/?utm_source=openai))

2) Which obligations apply next (what product teams should expect)
- The next big compliance wave is the AI Act’s full high‑risk regime (Annex III and key transparency requirements). Those obligations are scheduled to apply from 2 August 2026 unless and until the Commission/legislature alters the dates. Obligations in that wave include (among others): risk‑management systems, data governance and bias mitigation, technical documentation and logging, human‑oversight measures, conformity assessment (third‑party notified bodies where required), registration in the EU database, post‑market monitoring and incident reporting, and CE‑mark/declaring conformity where applicable. ([eur-lex.europa.eu](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/?utm_source=openai))

3) Key uncertainty and timing ambiguity product teams must keep in mind
- Standardisation and notified‑body capacity: the effective timing and practical ability to complete conformity assessments depends on availability of harmonised standards and notified‑body capacity. The Commission and stakeholders have flagged delays/capacity constraints, which could make it hard to complete third‑party assessments at scale by the statutory dates. Plan for constrained NB capacity and for having documentation ready early. ([medialaws.eu](https://www.medialaws.eu/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/SWD_2025_836_1_EN_autre_document_travail_service_part1_v6_QUrYKmjKRMWaKc6arEjzxHZL8is_121743.pdf?utm_source=openai))
- Member‑State readiness and uneven enforcement: national market‑surveillance / supervisory authorities and “notified bodies” are appointed by Member States; some Member States were slower to designate competent authorities, so enforcement readiness is uneven across the EU — expect variations in inspection and sanctioning practice early on. ([medialaws.eu](https://www.medialaws.eu/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/SWD_2025_836_1_EN_autre_document_travail_service_part1_v6_QUrYKmjKRMWaKc6arEjzxHZL8is_121743.pdf?utm_source=openai))
- Possible legislative change (Digital Omnibus) and timeline linking to standards: the Commission published a “Digital Omnibus on AI” proposal (19 Nov 2025) that would (if adopted) link the start of some high‑risk rules to availability of standards and could shift outer dates for certain categories (i.e., extend some start dates). That proposal is not law — it is under the ordinary legislative procedure and may be amended — so the statutory dates remain the starting point but are subject to political change. Treat the Omnibus as a plausible source of schedule change, not a certainty. ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/faqs/digital-package?utm_source=openai))
- Interpretive uncertainty (scope & definitions): practical questions remain about scope (what exactly counts as a “general‑purpose” model in specific product architectures, when a system is “high‑risk”, how the Act interacts with sectoral laws like the Medical Device Regulation). Expect guidance and delegated acts to refine definitions — but those instruments may arrive late. Product teams should map corner cases and document design decisions. ([eur-lex.europa.eu](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/?utm_source=openai))
- Enforcement focus and practical risk: initial enforcement is often prioritized toward the most harmful/egregious cases (bans already in force, clear violations of prohibited practices). However, firms should not rely on lax enforcement — fines and reputational damage are real and the Commission/AI Office have signalled active oversight for GPAI and high‑risk systems. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/api/files/document/print/en/ip_25_1787/IP_25_1787_EN.pdf?utm_source=openai))
