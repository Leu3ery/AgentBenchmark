Short answer (snapshot as of 15 March 2026)

1) Most recent enforcement milestone
- The most recent milestone that has already come into effect is the set of governance rules and the obligations for general‑purpose AI (GPAI) providers — these became applicable on 2 August 2025. ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai))
- (Earlier milestone: the AI Act’s list of prohibited practices became effective on 2 February 2025.) ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai))

2) Which obligations apply next
- The next major applicability date is 2 August 2026, when most remaining AI Act obligations (including the bulk of the high‑risk and transparency requirements) are scheduled to apply. ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai))
- Note the special/extended deadlines: certain high‑risk AI that is embedded in regulated products has an extended transition period until 2 August 2027. ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai))

3) Key uncertainties a product team must explicitly plan for
- Possible legislative change to the schedule (Digital Omnibus): the European Commission proposed a “Digital Omnibus” package (adopted by the Commission on 19 Nov 2025) that would adjust/simplify implementation (including targeted timeline adjustments for some high‑risk obligations). That proposal is still in the legislative process and is NOT law — relying on it to delay compliance is risky. ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/library/digital-omnibus-ai-regulation-proposal?utm_source=openai))
- Missing harmonised technical standards and delayed practical guidance: standardisation bodies (CEN/CENELEC) and some Commission guidance have lagged their target timelines, and the Commission missed at least one guidance deadline (early Feb 2026) that stakeholders expected for clarifying key definitions (e.g., high‑risk scope). The lack of completed standards and delayed guidelines creates real implementation ambiguity about how to demonstrate conformity (testing, metrics, documentation templates). ([clearact.net](https://clearact.net/de/articles/eu-ai-act-crisis-missed-deadlines-missing-standards-february-2026))
- Enforcement and market‑surveillance readiness is uneven across Member States: national competent authorities, notified bodies and the EU governance machinery are being stood up and resourced; this creates variation in inspection/enforcement timing and practice across countries. ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai))
- Definition / scope questions remain important: whether a system is a “GPAI”, a “high‑risk” system, or a provided-vs-deployed classification can materially change obligations — these hinge on guidance and (potentially) future delegated/implementing acts. Treat classification decisions as high‑stakes and document your reasoning. ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai))
- Interaction with GDPR and other EU laws: data‑protection and other sector rules remain an independent enforcement axis (EDPB/EDPS have already published views on related proposals), so compliance work must coordinate with privacy and product‑safety teams. ([edpb.europa.eu](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/plenary-meetings/115th-plenary-meeting-1_fr))

Practical implications / recommended near‑term actions for product teams
- Do not pause preparation waiting for the omnibus or guidance. Treat 2 Aug 2026 as the working deadline and use any delay as extra time to improve documentation. (This is the conservative, risk‑averse approach recommended by EU compliance commentators.) ([clearact.net](https://clearact.net/de/articles/eu-ai-act-crisis-missed-deadlines-missing-standards-february-2026))
- Immediately: inventory every AI system (including third‑party models), map roles (provider vs deployer), and classify likely risk tiers. Start or strengthen risk‑management, documentation, incident reporting and post‑market monitoring processes now. ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai))
- For GPAI and generative AI: adopt the public summary / transparency templates and the GPAI code of practice where available; the Commission has already published GPAI guidance/templates to help compliance. ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai))
- Track three parallel inputs closely: (a) the Commission’s implementing/delegated acts and guidance, (b) CEN/CENELEC harmonised standards as they are released, and (c) the Digital Omnibus legislative process and related opinions from the EDPB/EDPS. These will determine HOW you prove conformity. ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai))

Explicitly called‑out uncertainties / timing ambiguity (summary)
- Whether the Digital Omnibus will become law and in what form — it could shift deadlines or definitions, but it must pass the Parliament/Council process first (so current dates remain the safe assumption). ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/library/digital-omnibus-ai-regulation-proposal?utm_source=openai))
- Whether harmonised standards and Commission implementing guidance required to show conformity will be ready by 2 Aug 2026 — many stakeholders (and standard bodies) indicate delays, which creates uncertainty about the form of “acceptable” conformity evidence. ([clearact.net](https://clearact.net/de/articles/eu-ai-act-crisis-missed-deadlines-missing-standards-february-2026))
- Variation in national enforcement readiness and interpretation — expect uneven inspection/enforcement in the short term. ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai))
